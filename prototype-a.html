i lead design at clickview — an edtech company used by about 6,000 schools. i've been building and leading design teams for over six years now. started managing when i was 24, which was terrifying and probably the best thing that happened to me.

what do i actually do? i make it easier for teams to do good work. that sounds vague so let me be specific. i build frameworks, run workshops, write the kinds of documents most people skip — manifestos, principles, the stuff that sounds dramatic but actually just gives people a shared language.

i care about the messy parts. why someone doesn't speak up in a crit. why a process looks great on paper but nobody follows it. the gap between knowing something and actually doing it. that's where the interesting work is.

i believe clarity beats cleverness every time. that structure doesn't slow you down — it speeds you up. that you should build something once and reuse it instead of reinventing from scratch. that every trade-off should be intentional, not accidental. and that the quality of what you ship says something about the care you put in.

the team i'm proudest of is one that doesn't need me in the room. people who think for themselves, challenge each other, and keep raising the bar because they want to — not because someone told them to.
