# what happens to junior designers

one of the things i keep coming back to with AI: the tedious work it removes is often the same work that taught me how to think.

i'm not talking about the hours of pixel-pushing. i mean the stuff where you had to hold the whole problem in your head for long enough to understand it. writing the same component ten different ways until you had an intuition for which one was right. sitting with a messy brief until the shape of it became clear. being handed a dead-end task and figuring out why it was a dead end.

AI can do most of that faster than a junior can. which is useful. and also kind of a problem.

the thing about tedious work is that it's rarely just tedious. it's often where you build the internal models — about how systems hold together, about where the seams are, about what trade-offs feel like when you're the one making them. you can't shortcut to that. you accumulate it.

so what happens when the accumulation path disappears?

i've been trying to figure out what changes for how we develop people, now that the reps look different. the honest answer is i don't fully know yet. i've been experimenting — more structured crit, more explicit "here's my reasoning" narration, more time on the question of *why this decision, not just what*. it's early.

what i do think: the role of a senior designer shifts. less "here's how you do the thing" and more "here's how i evaluate whether the thing is right." that judgment — the part that knows when AI output is actually good versus when it just looks finished — that's the thing you can't skip.

the risk is we end up with designers who can prompt well but don't have a strong enough internal standard to catch the subtle wrong. the outputs will be confident. the misses will be invisible until they're not.

i don't have a framework for this. i'm watching my team closely and trying to name what i'm seeing as i see it. if you're leading a design team right now and you've worked out something that's actually helping, i'd genuinely like to hear it.
