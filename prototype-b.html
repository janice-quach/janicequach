i'd rather my team disagree with me than nod along.

nodding is easy. it feels like alignment but it's usually just silence. the best work i've been part of started with someone saying "i don't think that's right" — and us figuring out what was.

if it needs explaining, it's not clear enough.

i don't mean dumbing things down. i mean doing the work to make something land without a walkthrough. clarity isn't a nice-to-have, it's the design.

bringing people on the journey is not the same as getting buy-in.

buy-in is transactional. you present, they approve, everyone moves on. bringing people on the journey means they actually understand why — and they can carry it forward without you in the room.

frameworks don't change behaviour. they just make the gap visible.

i've built a lot of frameworks. the useful ones aren't the ones people follow — they're the ones that help people see what they're actually doing versus what they think they're doing.

i want to riff, build, disagree, and come out with something better.

that's it. that's the whole thing. give me people who want to think out loud, try stuff, break it apart, and make it sharper. everything else is just meetings.
