clarity over cleverness

if it needs explaining, it's not clear enough. i believe in principles that compound — simple rules that make every decision after them easier.

structure creates speed

clear systems reduce friction and compound over time. i don't build processes for process sake — i build them because the right structure means the team spends less time navigating and more time making.

every decision optimises something and sacrifices something. i'd rather say that out loud than pretend we can have it all.

build once, reuse often

solve in ways that scale, not just in ways that ship. the way something is made reflects how much you care about the people using it.

i think in systems. not because systems are elegant, but because the right system means the next person doesn't have to solve the same problem again.

intentional trade-offs

every decision optimises something and sacrifices something. the honest thing to do is say it out loud — not hide behind "best practice" or pretend trade-offs don't exist.

i'd rather make a clear decision with known costs than an unclear one that feels safe.

contribution over presence

i want us to riff, build, disagree, explore and come out with something better than any of us could've made alone.

what frustrates me most is when people don't bring their own thinking to the table. if i have to steer every decision or inject every idea, it stops being a team and starts being a funnel.

the gap between knowing and doing

everyone nods when you name the pattern. the hard part is what happens next. i care about closing that gap — turning frameworks into actual behaviour change, not just shared language.

awareness is not the same as action. i've run workshops where the whole room has the aha moment, and then nothing changes. that's the problem i keep coming back to.

decisions like a tree

some decisions are leaves — you make them. some are branches — bring them to me. some are the trunk — i get final say. knowing which is which is how autonomy works without chaos.

when to escalate: only after you've explored the options. show me what you've tried, not just what you're stuck on.
